Fed Hikes Rates Again, Squeezing Working Families Already Stretched Thin

The Federal Reserve pushed interest rates up another quarter point on Wednesday,
piling fresh pain onto households already struggling with soaring rents, credit
card debt, and crushing mortgage costs. Critics blasted the decision as tone-deaf,
warning that ordinary Americans — not Wall Street — will bear the brunt of yet
another hike.

"They keep punishing workers to fix a problem workers didn't cause," said a
labor advocate, pointing to record corporate profits as the real driver of price
increases. Housing affordability has collapsed in many cities, with first-time
buyers effectively locked out of the market. Small businesses say borrowing costs
are choking their ability to hire.

Economists warn that continued tightening risks tipping the economy into a
recession that would disproportionately hurt low-income workers and communities
of color. Chair Jerome Powell defended the move, but acknowledged the pain
being felt across Main Street.
