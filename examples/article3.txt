Fed Raises Rates 0.25 Points; Powell Cites Mixed Signals

The Federal Reserve raised interest rates by a quarter point on Wednesday,
bringing the federal funds rate to its highest level in over two decades.
Chair Jerome Powell described the decision as a balancing act between
ongoing inflation concerns and signs of cooling in parts of the economy.

Equity markets closed mixed. Treasury yields ticked higher. Powell said
the Fed would remain data-dependent and made no commitment about the path
of future rate decisions, citing uncertainty in housing, labor, and
consumer spending data.

Economists were divided on the implications. Some argued the move was
necessary to keep inflation expectations anchored; others said the cumulative
effect of past hikes was still working through the economy and that further
tightening risked overshooting. Powell acknowledged both views during the
press conference.
